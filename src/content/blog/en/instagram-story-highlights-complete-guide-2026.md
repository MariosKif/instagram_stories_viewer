## What Are Instagram Story Highlights?

Story Highlights are collections of past Stories that you pin to your profile. Unlike regular Stories that vanish after 24 hours, Highlights remain visible indefinitely until you manually remove them.

Each Highlight appears as a circular icon below your bio. You can give each one a custom name and cover image. When someone taps a Highlight, they view the saved Stories in sequence, just like watching regular Stories.

### Why Highlights Matter in 2026

Instagram profiles have become digital storefronts. When someone discovers your account — through a Reel, a tagged post, or a search — they visit your profile to decide whether to follow. Highlights give you a way to immediately communicate who you are, what you offer, and why they should care.

In 2026, with attention spans shorter than ever, the few seconds a visitor spends scanning your Highlights can determine whether they tap Follow or swipe away.

## How to Create Instagram Story Highlights

Creating Highlights is straightforward, but there are several approaches depending on whether you're working with existing Stories or new content.

### Method 1: Create From Your Archive

1. Go to your Instagram profile and tap the **+** icon below your bio (labeled "New")
2. Browse your Story Archive — every Story you've posted is saved here automatically
3. Select the Stories you want in the Highlight
4. Tap **Next**, name the Highlight, and choose a cover image
5. Tap **Add** to publish

### Method 2: Add From an Active Story

1. Open your currently active Story
2. Tap the **Highlight** icon at the bottom
3. Choose an existing Highlight to add it to, or create a new one
4. The Story is now saved to that Highlight immediately

### Method 3: Edit Existing Highlights

1. Long-press on any Highlight on your profile
2. Tap **Edit Highlight**
3. Add or remove Stories, change the name, or update the cover image
4. Tap **Done** to save

## How to Organize Highlights Strategically

Random Highlights hurt more than they help. Organization is everything.

### For Personal Brands and Creators

Organize Highlights by content category or theme:

- **About Me** — introduce yourself, share your story, explain what followers can expect
- **Best Of** — curate your most popular or impressive content
- **Tips** — educational content grouped by topic
- **Behind the Scenes** — personal, authentic moments that build connection
- **Testimonials** — screenshots of positive feedback, results, or social proof

### For Businesses and E-commerce

Structure Highlights to mirror a customer journey:

- **Products/Services** — showcase what you sell with clear visuals and pricing
- **How It Works** — explain your process, shipping, or onboarding
- **Reviews** — customer testimonials and user-generated content
- **FAQ** — answer common questions to reduce support inquiries
- **Sales/Offers** — current promotions and discount codes

### For Restaurants and Local Businesses

- **Menu** — full menu with photos
- **Hours & Location** — essential info visitors need
- **Events** — upcoming happenings
- **Reviews** — happy customer moments

### Highlight Naming Best Practices

Keep names short — only the first 10-15 characters are visible on most screens. Use clear, descriptive words rather than clever puns. Emojis work well as prefixes to add visual variety while keeping names concise.

## How to Design Professional Highlight Covers

Default Highlight covers pull from the first frame of the first Story in the collection, which often looks messy and inconsistent. Custom covers transform your profile from amateur to polished.

### Design Principles for Covers

- **Use a consistent style** — pick one aesthetic (icons on solid backgrounds, branded photos with overlays, or illustrated graphics) and apply it across all Highlights
- **Match your brand colors** — use the same 2-3 colors from your branding
- **Keep it simple** — covers are displayed as tiny circles, so complex designs become unreadable
- **Use icons or single letters** — a simple icon representing each Highlight's content is usually more effective than text

### Tools for Creating Covers

Canva remains the most popular choice in 2026 for designing Highlight covers. Create a 1080x1920 pixel image, center your icon or design element, and remember that Instagram will crop it into a circle. Free icon sets from sites like Flaticon provide clean, professional graphics.

### How to Set a Custom Cover

1. Edit the Highlight (long-press, tap Edit Highlight)
2. Tap **Edit Cover**
3. Choose an image from your camera roll (the cover image doesn't need to be part of the Highlight)
4. Position and scale the image within the circle
5. Tap **Done**

## Highlights Maintenance — Keeping Them Fresh

Highlights are not a "set and forget" feature. Outdated Highlights damage credibility.

### Monthly Review Checklist

- Remove Stories with outdated information (old prices, discontinued products, past events)
- Reorder Stories within each Highlight so the strongest content comes first
- Add new Stories from the past month that performed well
- Check that cover images still load properly and match your current branding
- Archive or delete Highlights for topics that are no longer relevant

### How Many Highlights Should You Have?

Most profiles work best with 5-7 Highlights. Fewer than 3 looks sparse. More than 10 becomes overwhelming and forces visitors to scroll horizontally, which many won't do. Focus on quality and relevance over quantity.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I see who viewed my Instagram Highlights?

You can see viewers for Highlights that contain Stories posted within the last 48 hours. After that window closes, viewer data is no longer available. This is the same behavior as regular Stories — Instagram removes viewer lists after 48 hours regardless of whether the Story is in a Highlight.

### Do Instagram Highlights affect the algorithm?

Highlights themselves don't directly influence the Feed or Explore algorithm. However, the engagement they generate (profile visits, time spent on your profile, taps, and replies) sends positive signals about your account's overall value. Well-organized Highlights also increase the likelihood that a profile visitor will follow you, which impacts all future algorithmic distribution of your content.

### Can I rearrange the order of my Highlights?

Instagram doesn't offer a direct drag-and-drop reordering feature. Highlights are ordered by the most recently edited one appearing first. To move a Highlight to the front, simply edit it — add and then remove a Story, or change and revert the cover — and it will jump to the first position. This workaround remains the only method in 2026.

### How many Stories can I add to a single Highlight?

There is no strict limit published by Instagram, but Highlights with more than 100 Stories tend to become sluggish and difficult for viewers to navigate. For the best user experience, keep individual Highlights to 20-30 Stories maximum and create additional Highlights for overflow content.
