## Method 2: Track Engagement Metrics That Matter

Not every metric carries the same weight. Some numbers look impressive but tell you nothing actionable.

### Tier 1 Facebook Analytics Metrics

- **Engagement rate.** Total interactions divided by reach gives you the percentage of people who took action on your content.
- **Share count.** Shares extend your organic reach more than any other interaction type because they put your content in front of new audiences.
- **Comment quality.** A post with 50 thoughtful comments outperforms one with 200 single-emoji reactions in algorithmic value.
- **Save rate.** When users bookmark your posts, the platform interprets this as a strong signal that your content provides lasting value.

## Method 5: Monitor Reach and Impressions With Facebook Analytics

Reach and impressions are the most commonly misunderstood metrics. They sound similar but measure very different things.

### Reach vs Impressions Explained

Reach counts unique accounts that saw your content. Impressions count total displays, including repeat views by the same person.

### Why the Ratio Matters

A healthy impressions-to-reach ratio falls between 1.5 and 2.5. Above 3.0 means your content reaches the same people repeatedly. Always separate organic and paid reach to evaluate true content performance.

## Common Facebook Analytics Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced marketers fall into traps when interpreting their data.

### Mistakes That Distort Your Results

- **Checking metrics daily and overreacting.** Weekly and monthly trends reveal truth. Daily fluctuations are noise.
- **Ignoring negative feedback signals.** Hide rates and unfollow counts matter. High negative feedback means your content is pushing people away.
- **Comparing unlike content types.** Video posts generate different engagement patterns than image posts. Compare formats against their own benchmarks.
- **Forgetting to filter by organic reach.** Including paid reach in your content analysis inflates numbers and hides organic weaknesses.
