## Tips for Effective Comment Browsing

### Focus on Recent Posts

Comments on posts more than a week old are rarely updated. For current sentiment and active discussions, focus on posts from the last 48 hours.

### Read Reply Threads

The most valuable insights are often buried in reply threads, not top-level comments. A customer complaint at the top level might have a detailed brand response three replies deep, including troubleshooting steps or policy explanations.

### Check Multiple Posts

A single post's comments can be an outlier. If you are researching a brand or creator, read comments across their last 10-15 posts to get a reliable pattern. Spot-checking one viral post will give you a skewed picture.

### Save What Matters

If you find comments that are useful for your research, take screenshots or copy the text. Comments can be deleted at any time by the commenter or the account owner. Private browsing through a tool like [IGStoryPeek](https://www.igstorypeek.com) does not save your browsing history, so you will need to capture anything important yourself.
