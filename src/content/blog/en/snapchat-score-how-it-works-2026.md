## What Is Snapchat Score?

Snapchat Score is a numerical value assigned to every Snapchat account. You can see your own score by tapping your Bitmoji or profile icon on the camera screen. You can view other users' scores by tapping on their profile in your friends list.

### Where to Find Your Score

Your score appears in two places: directly below your username on your profile screen, and as two smaller numbers (sent and received) when you tap on the main score. These two sub-scores represent the total number of Snaps you have sent and received since creating your account.

### What the Number Represents

At its core, Snapchat Score reflects your overall activity on the platform. It is a running total that only goes up — you cannot lose points. The score is tied to your account permanently and resets only if you create a new account entirely.

## How Snapchat Score Is Calculated

Snapchat has never published an official formula, but years of user testing and observation have produced a reliable understanding of the scoring system.

### Confirmed Point Sources

| Action | Points Earned | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sending a Snap (photo) | 1 point | Must be sent to an individual, not just posted |
| Sending a Snap (video) | 1 point | Same value as photo snaps |
| Receiving a Snap | 1 point | Earned when the snap is delivered |
| Posting a Story | 1 point | One point per story post |
| Opening a Snap | 0 points | Opening does not add to score |
| Sending a Chat message | 0 points | Text chats do not count |
| Group Snaps | 1 point total | Sending to a group counts as one snap |

### The Bonus Point System

Many users have observed occasional jumps in their score that do not match their sending and receiving activity. These appear to be bonus points that Snapchat awards for specific behaviors, including returning to the app after a period of inactivity, maintaining streaks, and adding new friends. The exact bonus values are not confirmed, but they typically range from 1-6 points.

### What Does NOT Affect Your Score

Several activities have no impact on your Snapchat Score despite widespread belief otherwise. Watching Spotlight videos, viewing Discover content, using Snap Map, and making video or voice calls do not contribute points. ## Snapchat Score Myths vs Reality

The internet is full of misinformation about Snapchat Score. Here are the most persistent myths and what the evidence actually shows.

### Myth: Sending Snaps to More People Gives More Points Per Snap

Sending the same Snap to 10 people gives you 10 points (one per recipient), not bonus multiplied points. There is no efficiency bonus for mass-sending. However, sending individual snaps to multiple friends is still the fastest legitimate way to accumulate points.

### Myth: Video Snaps Are Worth More Than Photo Snaps

Both photo and video snaps are worth one point each. There is no difference in scoring between the two formats, despite this myth persisting since 2018.

### Myth: Your Score Affects Who Sees Your Content

Snapchat Score has zero impact on content distribution. Unlike Instagram or TikTok, where engagement metrics influence reach, Snapchat delivers your Snaps and Stories to everyone you send them to or who has added you, regardless of your score.

### Myth: Third-Party Apps Can Boost Your Score

Any app or service claiming to boost your Snapchat Score is either a scam or violates Snapchat's Terms of Service. Using these tools risks account suspension or permanent banning. The only way to increase your score is through genuine platform activity.

## Strategies to Increase Your Snapchat Score Faster

While there are no legitimate shortcuts, you can optimize your behavior to accumulate points more efficiently.

### Send Snaps Instead of Chats

Every text chat you send is a missed scoring opportunity. When you want to communicate with friends on Snapchat, send a photo or video Snap instead of a text message. Each Snap earns a point while texts earn nothing.

### Maintain Daily Activity

Snapchat appears to award small bonuses for daily active use. Logging in daily and sending at least a few Snaps keeps your score climbing steadily. The bonus point system seems to reward consistency over sporadic bursts of activity.

### Use Stories Regularly

Each Story post earns a point, and you can post multiple Stories per day. This is an easy way to add several points daily without needing to send individual Snaps to each friend.

Time spent on Spotlight, Discover, or Snap Map does not contribute to your score. If increasing your score is a priority, allocate more of your Snapchat time to sending and receiving Snaps rather than consuming passive content.

### Does a Higher Score Matter?

Functionally, no. Your Snapchat Score does not unlock features, improve visibility, or provide any platform advantage. It is purely a vanity metric that reflects usage history. However, some users view high scores as a form of social proof, and some friend groups use score comparisons as a casual social activity.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can my Snapchat Score go down?

No. Snapchat Score only increases. There is no mechanism for losing points, even if you delete snaps, remove friends, or become inactive for extended periods. The score is a cumulative lifetime total.

### Why did my Snapchat Score suddenly jump by a large amount?

Sudden score jumps are usually caused by Snapchat's bonus point system. Returning to the app after a break, reaching certain milestones, or app updates that recalculate scores can all cause noticeable increases. Track these changes with [IGStoryPeek's Score Change Tracker](https://www.igstorypeek.com/snapchat/score-change-tracker) to identify the pattern.

### Does my Snapchat Score update in real time?

Not exactly. There is typically a short delay between your activity and the score update. The score usually refreshes within a few minutes, but during peak usage times or after server updates, the delay can be longer.

### Can other people see my Snapchat Score?

Yes, if they have added you as a friend. Anyone on your friends list can view your score by tapping on your profile. Non-friends cannot see your score. There is no way to hide your score from friends without removing them.

### Is there a maximum Snapchat Score?

There is no known maximum. Some users have scores exceeding 10 million, and the system continues to function at those levels. Snapchat has not indicated that any cap exists, so theoretically, the score can continue increasing indefinitely.
