## How the Snapchat Best Friends Algorithm Works

The Best Friends list is generated automatically based on your interaction patterns. You cannot manually select who appears on your list — the algorithm decides based on measurable activity.

### The Signals That Determine Rankings

Snapchat evaluates several interaction types to rank your friends:

1. **Snap frequency** — How many snaps you send and receive from each person. This is the primary signal. Direct photo and video snaps carry more weight than text chats.
2. **Recency of interaction** — Recent activity counts more than interactions from weeks ago. The algorithm applies a time-decay function so that current relationships are reflected accurately.
3. **Reciprocity** — Two-way interactions rank higher than one-sided ones. If you send 50 snaps to someone but they send you only 2, they are less likely to appear as a Best Friend.
4. **Chat activity** — Text conversations within Snapchat contribute to the ranking but carry less weight than snap exchanges.
5. **Streak maintenance** — Active Snap Streaks signal consistent interaction and influence Best Friend positioning.

### How Often the List Updates

The Best Friends list refreshes frequently — typically every few hours. A significant change in your snap patterns can shift your rankings within a single day. If you suddenly start snapping a new contact frequently, they can move into your Best Friends list within 24-48 hours of consistent interaction.

## Understanding Snapchat Friend Emojis

Snapchat assigns emojis next to friend names to indicate the nature of your relationship. These emojis are visible only to you and update automatically.

### Default Emoji Meanings

| Emoji | Name | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Yellow Heart | Besties | You are each other's number one Best Friend |
| Red Heart | BFF | You have been each other's number one for two weeks |
| Pink Hearts | Super BFF | You have been each other's number one for two months |
| Grimacing Face | Mutual Besties | Your number one Best Friend is also their number one |
| Sunglasses Face | Mutual BFs | One of your Best Friends is also one of their Best Friends |

### Customizing Friend Emojis

You can change the default emojis assigned to each relationship tier. Navigate to Settings, then Friend Emojis, and tap any emoji to select a custom replacement. This is purely cosmetic — it does not change how the algorithm ranks your friends or who appears on your list.

## Snapchat Plus Solar System: Planets Explained

Snapchat Plus subscribers get access to the Solar System feature, which provides a more granular ranking of your closest friends using a planetary metaphor.

### How Planets Map to Rankings

Each planet represents a position in your Best Friends list:

| Planet | Position | Distance from "Sun" |
|---|---|---|
| Mercury | #1 Best Friend | Closest |
| Venus | #2 Best Friend | Very close |
| Earth | #3 Best Friend | Close |
| Mars | #4 Best Friend | Moderate |
| Jupiter | #5 Best Friend | Mid-range |
| Saturn | #6 Best Friend | Further |
| Uranus | #7 Best Friend | Far |
| Neptune | #8 Best Friend | Furthest |

In this system, you are the "Sun" and your friends orbit around you based on their ranking. When you view a friend's profile and see a planet badge, it tells you where they rank in your Best Friends hierarchy.

### What the Planet Badge Tells You

The planet badge only shows your ranking of that friend — it does not reveal their ranking of you. If you see someone as Mercury (your number one), they might see you as Jupiter (their number five). ## How to Change Your Best Friends List

Since the Best Friends list is algorithm-driven, you cannot directly edit it. However, you can influence it through your behavior.

### Moving Someone Up the List

To move a friend higher in your Best Friends rankings:

- **Increase snap frequency.** Send and receive more photo and video snaps with that person daily.
- **Maintain a Snap Streak.** Consistent daily exchanges strengthen the ranking signal.
- **Have conversations.** Regular chats supplement snap activity and reinforce the connection signal.
- **Be consistent.** Sporadic bursts of activity are less effective than steady daily interaction.

### Moving Someone Down or Off the List

To remove someone from your Best Friends list:

- **Reduce interaction.** Stop snapping and chatting with them. Within a few days to a week, they will drop off your list as the recency signal decays.
- **Increase activity with others.** Snapping other people more frequently pushes the person down your rankings as others climb.
- **Block or remove.** Removing someone as a friend immediately removes them from your Best Friends list.

### Can You Hide Your Best Friends List?

Your Best Friends list on your profile is visible to your mutual friends. In 2026, Snapchat Plus subscribers can hide the Best Friends section from their profile entirely. Free users cannot hide the list but can influence its composition through their interaction patterns.

## Best Friends List Privacy Concerns

The Best Friends feature has always raised privacy questions because it reveals information about your relationship patterns.

### What Others Can See

- **Your profile Best Friends** — Anyone who views your profile can see your top Best Friends (unless hidden with Snapchat Plus).
- **Emojis** — Friend emojis are private to you. No one else can see which emoji Snapchat assigns to your relationship.
- **Planet rankings** — Only visible to the person viewing their own friend list. Others cannot see where they rank in your solar system.

### What Others Cannot See

- The exact frequency of your snaps with any individual
- Your complete ranked friend list beyond the visible Best Friends
- Changes to your Best Friends over time

## Common Best Friends List Issues and Fixes

### Best Friends Not Updating

If your Best Friends list seems stuck, it is usually because:

- Your interaction patterns have not changed significantly enough to trigger a re-ranking
- You are comparing short-term activity against long-term patterns that carry accumulated weight
- The list refreshes on Snapchat's server schedule, which may lag by a few hours

### Unexpected People Appearing on Your List

If someone you do not interact with frequently appears on your list, check whether:

- They have been sending you snaps that you have been opening (even without reciprocating, opening their snaps counts as interaction)
- Group snap activity is contributing to the ranking
- Your other Best Friends have reduced their interaction with you, causing others to rank higher by default

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many people can be on your Snapchat Best Friends list?

The visible Best Friends list on your profile shows up to eight people. The Snapchat Plus Solar System also ranks eight friends using the planet metaphor. Internally, Snapchat ranks more contacts than eight, but only the top eight are displayed and assigned planet badges or profile visibility.

### Can someone tell if they are on your Best Friends list?

Yes, if they view your Snapchat profile. Your Best Friends are displayed on your profile page to mutual friends. However, they cannot see their exact ranking position unless they have Snapchat Plus and check the planet badge. You can hide Best Friends from your profile with a Snapchat Plus subscription.

### Do group chats affect the Best Friends list?

Group chats have minimal impact on Best Friends rankings. The algorithm prioritizes direct one-on-one snap exchanges and conversations. Sending a snap to a group does not carry the same weight as sending a direct snap to an individual. ### What happens to Best Friends when you block someone?

When you block someone on Snapchat, they are immediately removed from your Best Friends list and all friend-related data is cleared. If you unblock them later and re-add them as a friend, the Best Friends ranking starts from scratch — previous interaction history is not restored.

### Can you have the same Best Friend emoji with multiple people?

No. The Yellow Heart (Besties) emoji can only appear next to one person — your number one Best Friend who also has you as their number one. However, other emojis like the Sunglasses Face (Mutual BFs) can appear next to multiple people since they indicate shared Best Friends rather than a unique top ranking.
