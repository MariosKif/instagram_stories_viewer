## Step 1: Audit Your Facebook Privacy Settings Dashboard

The Privacy Checkup tool is the fastest way to review your current privacy configuration in one place.

### Accessing the Privacy Checkup

Navigate to Settings and Privacy, then select Privacy Checkup. Facebook organizes this into five categories: who can see what you share, how people find you, your data settings, your ad preferences, and app permissions. Work through each category systematically.

### What the Dashboard Reveals

Most users discover their profile is far more exposed than they assumed. Default settings allow friends of friends to see your posts and third-party apps to access your data.

## Step 2: Control Who Sees Your Posts and Profile

Audience controls determine which groups can view your content.

### Post Visibility Settings

| Setting | Default Value | Recommended Value |
|---|---|---|
| Future posts | Friends of Friends | Friends |
| Past posts | Mixed audiences | Friends (use Limit Past Posts) |
| Profile info (birthday, city) | Public | Friends or Only Me |
| Friends list | Public | Only Me |
| Email address | Friends of Friends | Only Me |
| Phone number | Friends of Friends | Only Me |

### Locking Down Your Profile Information

Set every piece of profile information to "Friends" or "Only Me" individually. Navigate to your profile, click Edit Details, and adjust the audience for each item. Your privacy configuration for profile information should never remain on "Public." ## Step 3: Manage Search and Discovery Facebook Privacy Settings

These settings control whether strangers can find your profile through search engines and Facebook's own search.

### Search Engine Indexing

Navigate to Settings, Privacy, and disable "Do you want search engines outside of Facebook to link to your profile?" When enabled, your profile appears in Google search results.

### Contact-Based Discovery

Set both email and phone number lookup options to "Friends" or "Only Me" to prevent strangers from finding you through contact information from data breaches.

## Step 4: Review App Permissions and Third-Party Access

Third-party apps connected to your Facebook account represent one of the largest privacy vulnerabilities.

### Auditing Connected Apps

Go to Settings, Apps and Websites. Remove every app you do not actively use. For apps you keep, review exactly what permissions each one holds and revoke anything unnecessary.

### Platform API Controls

| Permission Type | Risk Level | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Public profile access | Low | Allow for trusted apps only |
| Friend list access | Medium | Revoke from all apps |
| Email access | Medium | Allow for essential apps only |
| Post on your behalf | High | Revoke immediately |
| Access photos | High | Revoke from all non-essential apps |

### Preventing Future App Access

Disable the "Apps, Websites, and Games" platform entirely if you do not use Facebook login on third-party sites. This is the most aggressive privacy change you can make on the platform. ## Step 5: Configure Ad Preferences and Facebook Privacy Settings for Data

Facebook's advertising system uses your data to target ads. These controls reduce what advertisers can access.

### Ad Targeting Controls

Navigate to Settings, Ads, Ad Preferences. Disable all options under "Ad settings" including ads based on partner data and social interactions in ads. The Off-Facebook Activity tool shows which websites send your browsing data to Facebook. Clear your history and toggle off future tracking. ## Step 6: Secure Your Account Against Unauthorized Access

Strong account security prevents unauthorized users from accessing your profile and altering your privacy controls.

### Two-Factor Authentication Setup

Enable two-factor authentication using an authenticator app rather than SMS. Navigate to Settings, Security and Login, Two-Factor Authentication and select "Authentication App."

### Login Alerts and Recognized Devices

Turn on login alerts for notifications when someone accesses your account from an unrecognized device. These measures complement your privacy configuration by preventing unauthorized access. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I reset all my facebook privacy settings to the most private options?

There is no single "reset to private" button. Use the Privacy Checkup tool under Settings and Privacy to work through each category. Set post audiences to Friends, disable search engine indexing, and remove third-party app access.

### Can people still see my profile after adjusting facebook privacy settings?

Your name and profile photo remain visible to everyone regardless of your settings. However, locking down these controls hides posts, friends list, personal details, and timeline from non-friends.

### Do these privacy controls apply to Messenger conversations?

Messenger has separate privacy controls from your main Facebook profile. Messenger settings control who can contact you and who sees your active status. Review both sets of settings for coverage.

### How often does Facebook change its privacy settings?

Facebook typically updates privacy options two to four times per year. Major redesigns sometimes move settings to new locations. Following the [IGStoryPeek blog](https://www.igstorypeek.com/blog) keeps you informed about privacy changes across all platforms.

### Will changing facebook privacy settings affect my existing posts?

Changing your default audience setting only affects future posts. To apply changes to older content, use "Limit Past Posts" under Settings, Privacy. This changes all past posts set to "Public" or "Friends of Friends" down to "Friends."

### Can I make my Facebook profile completely invisible?

Complete invisibility is not possible on Facebook. Your name and profile photo always remain searchable. However, maximizing your privacy controls makes your profile functionally invisible to non-friends.
